Materials & Equipment Checklist

Substrates
  • Symphony Smart Homes shirt blanks (light + dark colors)
  • DTF gang sheet 22×200, split-color, 156 sets ordered
Heat-press hardware
  • HTVRONT Auto Heat Press 2 (auto press, body) ordered
  • Hat / sleeve press ordered
  • Pressing pillow / pad (sleeve seam clearance)
Accessories
  • Teflon / parchment cover sheets
  • Heat-resistant tape
  • Fabric ruler & T-square (for placement)
  • Lint roller
  • Sharp scissors (cut transfers apart)
  • Flat work surface, well lit

Placement Specs

PlacementPositionNotes
Front circular logo Centered, ~3 in below collar seam Use white art on dark shirts, black art on light shirts
Back slogan Centered, ~4 in below collar seam "We Build Smart Homes That Just Work"
website line: symphonysh.com
Sleeve QR Centered on sleeve face, ~2 in above sleeve hem QR resolves to https://symphonysh.com
Always measure from the collar seam (not the neckband edge) and from the hem seam (not the cuff fold). Verify centering with a fabric ruler / T-square — eyeballing fails on patterned or curved necklines.

Troubleshooting & Failure Modes

SymptomLikely causeFix
Transfer lifts at edges after peel Under-pressed (time/temp/pressure) Re-press 5–10 s with Teflon over the design at recipe temp
Cracked or webbed ink after first wash Over-pressed or peeled too hot Reduce dwell by 2–3 s; respect cold/warm peel per film spec
Faint / patchy ink coverage Insufficient pressure or uneven platen Use pressing pillow; verify HTVRONT pressure setting; flatten substrate
Logo off-center on front Skipped ruler/T-square check Re-measure from collar seam, mark with heat-safe tape, re-position
Sleeve QR scuffed or warped Pressed over sleeve seam without pillow Insert pressing pillow inside sleeve so face is flat; re-press
Sheet won't cut cleanly between sets Scissors dull or cut line ignored Sharp scissors; cut on the gutter, not through art; never trim into bleed
Wrong color art on shirt Picked white art for light shirt or black for dark White art → dark shirts only. Black art → light shirts only. Verify before pressing.
QR scans to wrong URL or fails Damaged transfer or wrong cut Test scan on a phone before final QA; replace transfer if it fails twice

Step-by-Step SOP

  1. 1. Intake & preflight
    • Confirm shirt blank color, size, and count match the order.
    • Confirm DTF sheet is the SPLIT-COLOR 156-set sheet (not a prior version).
    • Lay sheet flat — no curl, no creases. If curled, weight it overnight before cutting.
    Accept when: blank counts match, sheet is identified, surface is flat, lint roller is ready.
  2. 2. Cut transfers
    • Cut along the gutters between shirt sets — never into the art bleed.
    • Per shirt: 1 front circular logo, 1 back slogan, 2 sleeve QRs (left + right).
    • Stack cut transfers into kits of (1 front + 1 back + 2 sleeve QRs) per shirt.
    Accept when: each kit has all 4 pieces, color (white vs black art) matches the target shirt color, no transfer is torn or scuffed.
  3. 3. Prep shirt
    • Lint-roll the shirt front, back, and both sleeves.
    • Smooth out wrinkles. Pre-press the shirt 3–5 s to remove moisture and creases.
    • Identify collar seam and hem seams — these are your placement references.
    Accept when: shirt is flat, dry, lint-free, with seams clearly visible.
  4. 4. Align front (circular logo)
    • Find vertical center between the shoulder seams.
    • Measure ~3 in down from the collar seam — mark top edge of design with heat-safe tape.
    • Place transfer art-side-up (already not mirrored). Tape one or two corners to lock position.
    Accept when: logo is visually centered, T-square confirms square to shoulder seams, top edge measures within ±¼ in of 3 in below collar.
  5. 5. Press front
    • Cover with Teflon / parchment.
    • Press at the DTF film's recommended temp / time / pressure (use HTVRONT Auto Heat Press 2 recipe).
    • Peel per film spec (cold or warm). Never peel hot if film is cold-peel.
    • Re-press 5–10 s with Teflon to set.
    Accept when: transfer is fully bonded, no lifted edges, surface looks uniform under angled light.
  6. 6. Align & press back (slogan)
    • Flip shirt; smooth flat on platen, back facing up.
    • Center between back shoulder seams; measure ~4 in down from collar seam.
    • Verify slogan reads "We Build Smart Homes That Just Work" with "symphonysh.com" beneath.
    • Tape, Teflon, press, peel, re-press — same recipe as front.
    Accept when: slogan is square to shoulders, centered ±¼ in, fully bonded, URL line readable.
  7. 7. Align & press sleeves (QR codes)
    • Slide sleeve over a pressing pillow / pad so the face is flat and seam is clear.
    • Center QR transfer side-to-side on the sleeve face.
    • Position bottom edge of QR ~2 in above the sleeve hem.
    • Press with Teflon at recipe temp; peel per film spec; re-press.
    • Repeat for the other sleeve.
    Accept when: QR is flat, square, undamaged, and a phone scans it to https://symphonysh.com on the first try.
  8. 8. Final QA
    • Inspect all 4 placements under good light and at an angle for lifted edges.
    • Run the QR test scan again after cooling.
    • Tug-test corners gently — nothing should peel.
    • Reject any shirt that fails any item; re-press or replace.
    Accept when: all checks pass; the shirt is wearable and ready to log.
  9. 9. Package & log
    • Fold per packaging standard; place in poly bag if shipping.
    • Log: date, operator, shirt size + color, sheet ID, any issues.
    • Move shirt to fulfillment / inventory bin.
    Accept when: shirt is packaged, the log entry is saved, and the workstation is reset for the next unit.

Final QA Checklist

  • Front logo centered, ~3 in below collar, square to shoulders
  • Back slogan centered, ~4 in below collar, "symphonysh.com" line legible
  • Both sleeve QRs flat, ~2 in above hem, undamaged
  • Phone scan of each sleeve QR resolves to https://symphonysh.com
  • No lifted edges on any of the 4 placements (angled-light check)
  • White art on dark shirts / black art on light shirts (color rule)
  • Tug test passed on all transfers
  • Shirt is lint-free, no scorch marks, no creases pressed in
  • Production log entry saved (date, size, color, operator, issues)

Future Automation Notes

  • Recipe store: persist temp / time / pressure per film + shirt blend so a robot or new operator just looks them up.
  • Vision-assisted alignment: a camera + reference fiducials on the platen could replace ruler/T-square steps 4, 6, and 7.
  • QR verify station: add a fixed scanner that auto-logs each sleeve scan instead of phone-checking by hand.
  • Run log: connect this tab to Cortex memory so per-shirt logs (size, color, issues) post automatically and feed defect-rate metrics.
  • Sheet inventory: track how many of the 156 sets are consumed; warn when re-order is due.
  • Hand-off: any human or future robot operator should be able to follow this SOP top-to-bottom without prior context.
