3. Pros
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Multi-channel dispatch is centralized in notification-hub.
- BlueBubbles gave the stack a proper programmable iMessage
  endpoint over Tailscale, not hacks.
- Approval flow has three valid entry points (dashboard, iMessage
  YES/NO, task-runner queue) — redundant but converges on the
  decision_journal.
- Message events flow into Cortex memory for later analysis.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------
4. Cons / weaknesses
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- No retry queue for failed sends. If notification-hub or
  imessage-server fails to deliver, the message is lost.
- No "test mode" for Telegram bots. Flipping a bot into dry-run
  requires env changes.
- No alerting when task_runner_health FAILS, email-monitor
  connection drops, or polymarket-bot loses VPN. These silent
  failures rely on Matt or an agent reading dashboards / logs.
  Hooking into notification-hub for DEGRADED-state alerts is the
  biggest leverage in this category.
- telegram-interface/ has unclear role vs telegram-bob-remote/ —
  second Telegram surface with no DECOMMISSIONED marker.
- ops:email_action publish claim needs verification (STATUS_REPORT
  + April 13 audit disagree).
- No outbound-comm audit log distinct from decision_journal — a
  per-message ledger would help on legal / compliance questions.
