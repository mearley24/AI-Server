## Report Types

### 1. Competitive Analysis Reports
**What it looks like:**
- "Compare these 5 CRM vendors for a mid-market real estate brokerage"
- "Analyze the competitive landscape of the smart home automation market"
- "Compare 3 payroll software options for a 50-person professional services firm"

**Standard structure:**
```
Title: [Topic] Competitive Analysis — [Year]

1. Executive Summary (200–300 words)
   - Market context
   - Competitors covered
   - Key findings (3 bullets)
   - Recommendation

2. Methodology (100–150 words)
   - Data sources used
   - Evaluation criteria
   - Limitations

3. Competitor Profiles (300–500 words each)
   - Company overview
   - Product/service description
   - Pricing and packaging
   - Strengths and weaknesses
   - Ideal customer profile

4. Side-by-Side Comparison (table)
   - 8–12 criteria rows
   - Each competitor rated or described per criterion

5. Findings and Analysis (300–400 words)
   - Market positioning map
   - Category leaders vs. challengers
   - Key differentiators

6. Recommendation (150–200 words)
   - Best choice for client's stated needs
   - Rationale
   - Caveats

7. Sources
   - All URLs and data sources cited
```

**Time estimate:** 60–90 minutes  
**Rate target:** $200–400 on Upwork; $250–600 for direct clients

### 2. Market Research Reports
**What it looks like:**
- "What's the size and growth rate of the US smart home market?"
- "Research the real estate investment market in Phoenix, AZ for 2026"
- "Overview of the freelance platform market: key players, trends, sizing"

**Standard structure:**
```
Title: [Market] Market Research Report — [Year]

1. Executive Summary
2. Market Definition and Scope
3. Market Size and Growth
   - Current market size (with source)
   - Historical growth rate
   - Projected CAGR and forecast period
4. Market Segmentation
   - By product/service type
   - By geography
   - By customer segment
5. Key Players
   - Top 5–10 companies with brief profiles
   - Market share estimates if available
6. Trends and Drivers
   - Technology trends
   - Regulatory factors
   - Consumer behavior shifts
7. Challenges and Risks
8. Opportunities
9. Conclusion
10. Sources
```

**Time estimate:** 75–120 minutes  
**Rate target:** $250–500

### 3. Due Diligence Reports
**What it looks like:**
- "Research this company before we consider acquiring them"
- "Background check on this potential partner company"
- "Summarize this company's financial health and online reputation"

**Standard structure:**
```
1. Executive Summary
2. Company Overview
   - Founded, headquarters, leadership
   - Products/services
   - Revenue (if public or estimable)
3. Financial Health Indicators
   - Funding history (Crunchbase)
   - Revenue estimates (if available)
   - Burn rate indicators
4. Reputation Analysis
   - News mentions (last 12 months)
   - Review site scores (G2, Trustpilot, etc.)
   - Legal/regulatory issues
5. Competitive Position
6. Risk Factors
7. Conclusion
8. Sources
```

**Time estimate:** 45–90 minutes  
**Rate target:** $150–350

## Research Protocol

### Data Sources (in priority order)
1. **Primary sources:** SEC filings, government databases (Census, BLS, FRED), company IR pages
2. **Premium research:** Gartner, IDC, Forrester summaries available via press releases and executive summaries
3. **News sources:** WSJ, Bloomberg, Reuters, industry trade publications
4. **Aggregators:** Crunchbase, PitchBook (public data), LinkedIn
5. **Review platforms:** G2, Capterra, Trustpilot for competitive intelligence

### Citation Format
All reports must cite sources. Use inline citations [1] with a numbered sources list at the end.

```
[1] Author. "Title." Publication, Date. URL
[2] Company. "Report Name." Year. URL
```

### Fabrication Policy
**Never fabricate statistics.** If a specific number is unavailable, use an approximation range and source it explicitly: "Estimated $X–Y billion based on [source]."
