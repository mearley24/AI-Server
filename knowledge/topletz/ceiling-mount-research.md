---

## Critical Terminology Note: "360° Swivel" in Ceiling Mounts

There are two fundamentally different types of "360° rotation" in ceiling mounts:

1. **Installation-position swivel (set-and-lock):** The column/mount can be rotated to any angle *during installation*, then locked with set screws. Common in Chief Fusion XCM series and Peerless PLCM series. Not adjustable after installation without tools.

2. **True free-rotation swivel:** The display rotates continuously around the pole post-install, like a pole-mounted display you can spin. Used in Atdec TH-3070 series. Can be optionally locked.

**For a sloped ceiling near a hearth wall, free-rotation is ideal** — it lets you fine-tune the TV's facing angle after installation and swing it away from the wall safely.

## Recommended Path

### Primary Recommendation: Chief XCM7000 (via ADI)
The XCM7000 is the strongest match available through established US integrator channels. Key considerations:

- **Weight safety:** 300 lb capacity / 137 lb TV = 2.19× factor — appropriate for a ceiling mount
- **VESA:** Native 800×400 support, no adapter needed
- **TV size:** Rated to 100"
- **Dual-pole:** More stable than single-pole for a TV this size/weight
- **Channel:** Available at ADI Global (part # CJ-XCM7000)
- **Caveat on swivel:** This is NOT a free-rotation mount. The TV direction is set during installation, then locked. For the sloped ceiling/hearth wall situation, the installer must determine the exact desired TV orientation before final tightening of the set screws. This is standard practice in commercial installs and is workable — it just requires planning.

**Total system cost estimate:** Mount head ~$650–$750 + ceiling plate ~$50 + adjustable extension column ~$100–$150 = **~$800–$950 total**

### Alternative: Chief XCM1U (via ADI/Wave Electronics)
Same swivel behavior as XCM7000 but single-pole. At 250 lb / 137 lb = 1.82× factor, acceptable but the XCM7000 is preferred for ceiling installations where gravity loads are compounded by any dynamic forces.

### If True Post-Install Swivel is Required
No single product cleanly satisfies all requirements simultaneously. Two paths:

1. **Atdec TH-3070-CTL + weight verification:** If Hisense 100U8Q actual weight (without stand) is ≤110–120 lbs (some sources suggest ~124 lbs; official spec lists 137 lbs — verify actual shipment weight), the Atdec's 143 lb cap becomes viable. Contact Atdec (atdec.com) to confirm the VESA 800×400 compatibility with their rail system. Free rotation is the major advantage for hearth wall clearance management.

2. **Vogel's PUC 25 Dual-Pole:** Best all-around specs but US distribution requires direct engagement with Vogel's North America. Worth a call to Vogel's US office before dismissing.

## Notes on Sloped Ceiling / Hearth Wall Clearance

Given the TV is installed on a sloped ceiling near a hearth/mantle wall, the following should be considered regardless of mount chosen:

1. **Drop column length:** On a sloped ceiling, a longer drop column positions the TV farther from the ceiling plane, reducing the arc radius when the TV faces different directions. Chief's CPA adjustable columns (e.g., CPA-C10A or similar) can accommodate this.

2. **Chief angled ceiling adapter:** Chief makes sloped/angled ceiling plates (e.g., the CPA-CAP or similar) that allow mounting to pitched ceilings. If the slope angle is known, specify this to the distributor.

3. **Swing clearance calculation:** For a set-position mount like the Chief, calculate in advance: at the desired orientation, will any part of the TV's perimeter (particularly the side closest to the wall) be within safe distance from the mantle/wall? A 100" TV is approximately 88" wide and 51" tall. With the VESA centered on the mount, the TV extends 44" left and right of center, and ~25" above and below.

4. **Atdec free-rotation advantage for this use case:** The Atdec's true post-install free rotation would allow the installer (or homeowner) to physically rotate the TV away from the wall after installation — but at 143 lb capacity, the safety margin is thin. Use with caution and only if the actual unboxed TV weight is confirmed substantially below 137 lbs.

No new pro-grade ceiling mount models meeting all criteria (360° + 800×400 VESA + 150+ lb + 100" + US integrator channel) were identified as released in 2025 or 2026.
